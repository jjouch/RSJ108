How to Prepare for an Interview

Assembly time: 30 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Have you ever needed to conduct an interview but weren't sure where to start? Whether it's for school or professionally, this guide will help you get started!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Pen
  • Paper
  • Computer
  • Internet
  • Recording Device

  1. Research the person.

    You should know as much as possible about the person you're interviewing so you go into the interview feeling prepared and in control. Look up as much relevant information about the person as possible, and try to find some recordings of in-person interviews with that person.

  2. Clarify your objective.

    Before you prepare your interview questions, you should understand your objective for interviewing the person. Is it simply to give readers more insight into that person's personal life, to discuss one aspect of that person's career, or to discuss that person's perspective on a certain situation? Your questions need to stay on topic with that objective.

    Take detailed notes!

  3. Prepare questions.

    You should prepare questions that are flexible and allow for other questions after. Don't ask any "yes" or "no" questions or questions that you could google. Only ask just one question at a time. Ask questions that are relevant to your objectives. Don't ask too broad of questions.

  4. Prepare some topics for small talk.

    Prepare your small talk before the interview to make them comfortable. The subject will feel more at ease at the start of the interview if they can talk about something that makes them comfortable.

  5. Check your recording equipment.

    If you're using a recording device, make sure it is working before the interview It is very awkward is you are not prepared.
